# Day 30 – Supply Chain Optimizer

## Project
Built an interactive Supply Chain Optimizer simulator using a single HTML file. The application helps users understand supply chain concepts by making real business decisions and observing their impact on key business metrics.

## Overall Supply Chain Score
**85/100 – Excellent Supply Chain**

## Company Profile
- Company: Aster Group
- Industry: Furniture
- Countries Served: 3

## Decisions Taken
- Suppliers: Multiple Suppliers
- Factory Location: Nearshore
- Warehouse Strategy: Regional Network
- Transportation: Rail
- Inventory Strategy: Balanced

## Final Business Metrics
- Cost: 28/100
- Delivery Speed: 89/100
- Risk: 7/100
- Customer Satisfaction: 88/100
- Sustainability: 83/100

## Key Learnings
- Supply chain optimization requires balancing cost, speed, risk, and customer satisfaction.
- Multiple suppliers improve resilience against disruptions.
- Nearshore manufacturing provides a good balance between cost and operational control.
- Regional warehouses improve delivery speed and customer satisfaction.
- Rail transportation is a sustainable and cost-effective logistics option.
- Balanced inventory helps avoid stock shortages while reducing unnecessary storage costs.

## Biggest Risk
Cost efficiency remains the primary challenge. Better supplier negotiations and shipment consolidation can further reduce operational expenses.

## Practical Improvements
1. Introduce order tracking and realistic delivery estimates.
2. Shift more transportation volume to rail or sea where possible.
3. Consolidate shipments and negotiate supplier contracts to reduce costs.
