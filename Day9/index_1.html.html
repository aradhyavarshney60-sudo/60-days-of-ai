Reference

Nutrition sources & disclaimer

Where the numbers in NutriScope come from, and the limitations you should know about.

Data sources

Food composition data — per-100g macro and micronutrient values are approximate figures compiled from public nutrition composition references such as the USDA FoodData Central database and typical published values for common Indian foods and dishes. Actual values vary by brand, variety, ripeness, and cooking method.

Calorie targets — estimated using the Mifflin-St Jeor Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) equation combined with standard Physical Activity Level (PAL) multipliers.

Micronutrient targets (RDA) — approximated from general adult Recommended Dietary Allowances referenced by bodies such as ICMR-NIN (Indian Council of Medical Research – National Institute of Nutrition) and WHO/FAO nutrient intake guidance. Individual needs vary by age, health status, pregnancy/lactation, and more.

Sodium guidance — follows the WHO-recommended upper intake limit of approximately 2,300 mg/day (roughly one teaspoon of salt).

Risk analysis logic — simple rule-based thresholds (e.g. <70% of target) commonly used in nutrition education materials to flag potential areas of concern; it is not a validated clinical screening tool.

Known limitations

Data reflects a single day's log and does not account for long-term dietary patterns, absorption differences, or nutrient interactions.

All data lives in your current browser session only — nothing is saved once you close or refresh the page.

Targets are generic and do not adjust for medical conditions, medications, allergies, pregnancy, lactation, or athletic performance goals.

Cooking method, added oil/salt/sugar, and portion estimation can meaningfully shift real nutrient values away from these averages.

Educational disclaimer

NutriScope is an educational nutrition-awareness tool. It is not a medical device, does not provide medical or dietetic advice, and must not be used to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease or health condition. Estimates shown are generalized and may not be accurate for your individual physiology or health circumstances. Always consult a registered dietitian, nutritionist, or physician before making significant changes to your diet — particularly if you are pregnant, nursing, managing a medical condition, or taking medication.
